3scouts · Deep Analysis Report

Georgian Hallmarked Silver Baby's Rattle with Coral Teething Attachment

Submitted by subscriber · June 2026

1. Item Identification

A fine Georgian silver baby's rattle and teether, comprising a whistle, four silver bells, a turned coral teething attachment and a ring for suspension. Fully hallmarked. The coral attachment confirms a pre-1840 date of manufacture. The form is consistent with English work from the Regency period, circa 1810–1830. … *

2. Authenticity Assessment

The hallmarks appear clear and consistent with the period. The coral is original and unrestored — a significant indicator of authenticity as replacements are common. Construction is consistent with hand-finishing typical of the early 19th century. Authenticity confidence: 88%. … *

3. Condition Assessment

Silver body: Grade B — light surface wear consistent with age, no dents or repairs visible. Bells: Grade A — all present, good resonance. Coral: Grade B+ — intact with minor surface crazing typical of age. Ring: Grade A.

Overall grade: B+ — excellent condition for age. … *

4. Comparable Sales

Similar Georgian silver rattles with coral have sold at auction as follows: Bonhams London (2024) — £680; Christie's South Kensington (2023) — £920; Lyon & Turnbull (2024) — £440; Woolley & Wallis (2023) — £1,200 (premier maker identified); eBay (2025) — £380 (hallmarks unclear). … *

5. Valuation

Estimated fair value: £450 — £750. The wide range reflects uncertainty about the maker. An unidentified provincial silversmith places the piece at the lower end. Identification as a premier London maker — Paul Storr, Hester Bateman, Matthew Boulton — would move the value dramatically toward the upper range or beyond. … *

6. Recommendation

This is a significant antique that deserves proper attention. The next step is to have the hallmarks examined under magnification by a specialist silver dealer or major auction house. That single piece of research could define the value precisely. Do not sell without this step. … *

* This is a much abbreviated version of the full report under each section.
